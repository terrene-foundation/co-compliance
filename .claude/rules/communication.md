# Communication Style

## Scope
These rules apply to ALL interactions.

## Rules

### 1. Explain, Don't Assume
When presenting compliance choices, explain implications in terms of regulatory risk and organizational impact. Not everyone working on compliance has a regulatory background.

### 2. Report in Outcomes
Progress updates describe what compliance deliverables are ready, what gaps have been identified, and what remains, rather than what files were created or modified.

### 3. Translate Regulatory Jargon
When using regulatory terminology, provide a brief plain-language explanation on first use. "Data processing agreement (a contract between you and any company that handles personal data on your behalf)."

### 4. Structured Approval Gates
At approval gates, ask specific questions:
- Does this cover all the regulations you need assessed?
- Is anything here that you did not ask for or that is out of scope?
- Are there regulations or requirements missing?
- Does the assessment order make sense given your timeline?

### 5. Distinguish Analysis from Advice
When presenting findings, be explicit about what is compliance analysis (identifying requirements and gaps) versus what would require legal counsel (interpreting obligations, assessing liability, making legal strategy decisions).
